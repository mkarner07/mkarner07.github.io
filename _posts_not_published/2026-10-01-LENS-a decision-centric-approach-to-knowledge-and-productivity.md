LENS is the system I use to turn the flood of meetings, emails and documents in my day-to-day work into something I can actually act on. It grew out of [TORP](https://matthiaskarner.com/2023/11/The-Total-Recall-Productivity-Method/), my earlier attempt at a "remeber everything" system, once I realized that recall wasn't the bottleneck anymore. Decisions were.

The core idea: most productivity systems optimize for storing and retrieving information. LENS optimizes for something else: **clarity under uncertainty**.

It's built around a few simple principles (insights > raw information, context > completeness, decisions as the real output) and a layered structure that goes from a highly condensed "what matters right now" view down to the raw material behind it.

I've been running this system for a few months now.

The core (the principles and the five-layer structure) has settled and I don't expect it to change much going forward.

What's still evolving is the outside: how information gets in, how AI fits into the workflow, and how the system connects to the tools I actually use day to day.

## Where LENS is still moving

The core of LENS (the principles and the layered structure) is stable at this point.

What's still in motion is everything at the edges: how AI agents fit into postprocessing and weekly review, which tools I use to run it and how much of the capture step can be automated without losing the "interpret, don't just store" discipline the system depends on.

I'll keep updating this page as that settles, but I don't expect the core described above to change much.
